# Reliance Industries · Israel-Iran-USA War · Hormuz Closure Deep-Dive

**Scenario assumption**: Israel-Iran-USA escalation triggers full or partial closure of the Strait of Hormuz for **45 days** at **severity 0.92**, with **Brent at $152/bbl**.

**Output**: 30-day revenue-at-risk across all 10 RIL conglomerate subsidiaries.

**Aggregate impact**: ₹**24,340 Cr** (point estimate) · band ₹5,948 – ₹30,348 Cr · **2.143% of FY24 RIL consolidated revenue (₹11,35,920 Cr)**.

> ⚠ Honest framing: this is a *conditional* projection — what happens *if* Hormuz closes for 45 days at this severity. We do NOT predict whether the closure will actually happen. We quantify second-order industrial effects assuming it does.

---

## TIER 1 — IMMEDIATE SHOCK NODES (1–7 days)

### #1 — Reliance Industrial Infrastructure Limited (RIIL)
- **Score 0.916** (highest) · Hormuz dependency **85%** · FY24 revenue ₹68 Cr · 30-day at-risk **₹35 Cr**
- **Why ranked #1**: pure pipeline + tankage operator; literally everything they do is on the Sikka-Jamnagar petroleum corridor.
- **Feedstock chain**: Crude / petroleum-product pipelines connecting Sikka VLCC port → Jamnagar refinery → Sikka petroleum-product loading → coastal tankers.
- **First symptom (day 4)**: pipeline throughput cut proportional to refinery utilisation cut; tankage utilisation rises 30–50% as inventory builds during slate-mix.
- **Citation**: BSE filings + RIIL FY24 annual report.

### #2 — Jamnagar Refinery (DTA + SEZ)
- **Score 0.824** · Hormuz dep **62%** · FY24 revenue **₹4,64,035 Cr** · 30-day at-risk **₹12,194 Cr**
- **Why ranked #2**: world's largest single-location refinery (1.4 million bbl/day combined). Crude slate ~60% Middle East with ~70% of that transiting Hormuz per FY24 disclosures.
- **Feedstock chain**: Saudi Aramco / ADNOC / Iraq SOMO crude → Hormuz transit → Sikka VLCC moorings → Jamnagar DTA + SEZ towers → diesel / petrol / ATF / petchem feedstock → domestic + export.
- **First symptom (day 4)**: VLCC tanker insurance premiums spike 5–10x within 48h; cargo rerouting via Cape adds 18–22 days; refinery utilisation cut 5–12% if heavy-medium crude slate cannot rebalance.
- **Citation**: RIL Integrated Annual Report FY24.

### #3 — RIL Petrochemicals (Hazira + Dahej + Vadodara)
- **Score 0.768** · Hormuz dep **48%** · FY24 revenue **₹1,52,400 Cr** · 30-day at-risk **₹3,907 Cr**
- **Why ranked #3**: naphtha cracker is downstream of Jamnagar feedstock; PX-PTA spread compression hits paraxylene economics.
- **Feedstock chain**: Hormuz crude → Jamnagar feedstock → naphtha cracker → ethylene / propylene / paraxylene → PE / PP / PTA → Hazira + Dahej + Vadodara downstream → polymer exports + domestic.
- **First symptom (day 7)**: naphtha price climbs 18–25% within 7 days; PX-MEG spread compresses; downstream PE/PP customer renegotiations begin.
- **Citation**: RIL O2C segment disclosures + ICIS naphtha/PX market data.

## TIER 2 — MID-CYCLE NODES (10–21 days)

### #4 — Reliance Power
- **Score 0.744** · Hormuz dep **42%** · FY24 revenue **₹7,841 Cr** · 30-day at-risk **₹190 Cr**
- **Feedstock chain**: domestic gas allocation (APM + HPHT + market) + R-LNG imports (95% Qatar via Hormuz) → gas-fired generation → state DISCOM PPAs.
- **First symptom (day 14)**: gas-fired plant load factor (PLF) cut 15–30% within 21 days; tariff renegotiation requests filed with state regulators.
- **Citation**: R-Power FY24 + CEA + PNGRB gas allocation reports.

### #5 — RIL Polyester / Recron Malaysia
- **Score 0.728** · Hormuz dep **38%** · FY24 revenue **₹42,800 Cr** · 30-day at-risk **₹861 Cr**
- **Feedstock chain**: Hormuz / Asia paraxylene (PX) → PTA → PSF + PFY → textile mills (Tirupur / Surat / Coimbatore) → garment exports to US/EU.
- **First symptom (day 12)**: PX import landed cost rises 15–22%; PTA-PX spread compresses; textile mills reduce PSF/PFY offtake.
- **Citation**: ICIS PX market data + RIL O2C polyester disclosures.

### #6 — Reliance E&P (KG-D6)
- **Score 0.700** · Hormuz dep **35%** · FY24 revenue **₹24,586 Cr** · 30-day at-risk **₹372 Cr**
- **Counter-intuitive note**: domestic gas pricing indexed to JKM-LNG benchmark; benchmark spike is a near-term *tailwind* for E&P realisations, BUT global recession risk caps demand later.
- **First symptom (day 10)**: JKM (Japan-Korea Marker) benchmark jumps 30–60% within 14 days; RIL-BP KG-D6 realisation rises proportionally on next pricing cycle.
- **Citation**: DGH + RIL E&P operatorship disclosures.

### #7 — Reliance General Insurance
- **Score 0.668** · Hormuz dep **22%** · FY24 GWP **₹10,989 Cr** · 30-day at-risk **₹426 Cr**
- **Counter-intuitive note**: war-risk premium *revenue* climbs 5–10x BUT marine claim payouts also climb 2–4x. Combined ratio typically deteriorates 8–15 percentage points during chokepoint events.
- **First symptom (day 2)**: war-risk premium quotes triple within 24h; marine claim notifications from rerouted cargo damage spike within 2 weeks.
- **Citation**: IRDAI public disclosures + Lloyd's war-risk index.

## TIER 3 — CONSUMER CASCADE NODES (21–45 days)

### #8 — Reliance Retail (RRVL)
- **Score 0.648** · Hormuz dep **18%** · FY24 revenue **₹3,06,848 Cr** · 30-day at-risk **₹4,569 Cr**
- **Lagging indicator**. Symptoms appear AFTER fuel-price pass-through completes (typically 21–35 days post-shock).
- **Feedstock chain**: consumer fuel + LPG + gas + electricity inflation cascade → household disposable income compression → consumer staples + discretionary footfall reduction → Reliance Retail SSSG slows.
- **First symptom (day 30)**: same-store-sales-growth softens 2–4 percentage points within 30 days; discretionary categories (Trends, Digital) hit harder than staples.
- **Citation**: RRVL FY24 disclosures + Reuters India consumption data.

### #9 — Jio Platforms
- **Score 0.616** · Hormuz dep **12%** · FY24 revenue **₹1,19,791 Cr** · 30-day at-risk **₹1,259 Cr**
- **Feedstock chain**: network equipment imports (radios, towers, fibre, switches) → shipping container freight rates → 5G rollout capex schedule. Hormuz indirect: container freight indexes spike when crude rises.
- **First symptom (day 21)**: 5G rollout capex cycle delayed 2–6 weeks; equipment vendor renegotiations on USD-denominated contracts; ARPU pressure if consumer cascade causes plan downgrades.
- **Citation**: Jio Platforms FY24 + Drewry Container Index.

### #10 — Network18 / Viacom18 / JioCinema
- **Score 0.592** · Hormuz dep **8%** · FY24 revenue **₹6,562 Cr** · 30-day at-risk **₹148 Cr**
- **Most distant cascade**. Symptoms LAG retail SSSG by 2–3 weeks.
- **Feedstock chain**: consumer fuel/LPG inflation → discretionary spending compression → FMCG advertiser budgets cut → TV+digital ad revenue softens → Network18 + Viacom18 + JioCinema affected.
- **First symptom (day 35)**: FMCG ad-spend reductions reach Network18/Viacom18 within 30–45 days; JioCinema sponsorship renewals delayed.
- **Citation**: Network18 FY24 + Pitch Madison ad-spend report.

## CONCLUSION

The Israel-Iran-USA Hormuz closure scenario at severity 0.92 / Brent $152 / 45-day duration costs RIL roughly **₹24,000 Cr in 30-day at-risk revenue** — about **2.1% of FY24 consolidated revenue**.

The damage concentrates in three nodes:
1. **Jamnagar Refinery** (50% of total at-risk) — through-the-roof crude slate cost and rerouting demurrage
2. **Reliance Retail** (19% of total at-risk) — consumer cascade from fuel inflation
3. **RIL Petrochemicals** (16% of total at-risk) — naphtha-PX-PTA spread compression

The remaining seven subsidiaries collectively account for ~15% of impact.

**Key insight**: highest *score* node (RIIL pipelines 0.916) has lowest *absolute* impact (₹35 Cr) because it is a small-revenue stub. Highest *absolute* impact (Jamnagar ₹12,194 Cr) has lower score (0.824) but the largest revenue base. **Score and absolute impact tell different stories — both matter.**
